K2-18b:

K2-18b is an exoplanet, which means it is a planet outside our Solar System. It is about 124 light-years away from Earth in the constellation Leo. The planet is around 2.6 times larger than Earth and about 8.6 times more massive. It orbits its red dwarf star, K2-18, every 33 days and is located in the star's habitable zone, where temperatures could allow liquid water to exist. Scientists think K2-18b may have a rocky core with a thick atmosphere that is mostly made of hydrogen. It may also have a deep ocean beneath its atmosphere, although this has not been confirmed.

K2-18b was discovered in 2015 by Canadian astronomers using data from NASA's Kepler Space Telescope during its K2 mission. Scientists found it by using the transit method, which detects tiny drops in a star's brightness when a planet passes in front of it. Later, the Hubble Space Telescope studied the planet's atmosphere and found evidence of water vapour. More recently, the James Webb Space Telescope has examined the atmosphere in even greater detail, helping scientists learn more about what gases are present.

Scientists think K2-18b is one of the most interesting planets to study because it could have some of the conditions needed for life. Water vapour, carbon dioxide and methane have been detected in its atmosphere, which are important ingredients for life as we know it. However, there is no proof that life exists there. Some observations have suggested the possible presence of a gas called dimethyl sulfide (DMS), which on Earth is mainly produced by living organisms, but scientists have not confirmed that it is really there. At the moment, K2-18b is considered a promising place to search for life, but much more research is needed before any conclusions can be made.
